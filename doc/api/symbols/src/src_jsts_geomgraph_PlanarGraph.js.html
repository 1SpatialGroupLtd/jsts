  9 /**
 10  * The computation of the <code>IntersectionMatrix</code> relies on the use of a structure
 11  * called a "topology graph".  The topology graph contains nodes and edges
 12  * corresponding to the nodes and line segments of a <code>Geometry</code>. Each
 13  * node and edge in the graph is labeled with its topological location relative to
 14  * the source geometry.
 15  * <P>
 16  * Note that there is no requirement that points of self-intersection be a vertex.
 17  * Thus to obtain a correct topology graph, <code>Geometry</code>s must be
 18  * self-noded before constructing their graphs.
 19  * <P>
 20  * Two fundamental operations are supported by topology graphs:
 21  * <UL>
 22  *   <LI>Computing the intersections between all the edges and nodes of a single graph
 23  *   <LI>Computing the intersections between the edges and nodes of two different graphs
 24  * </UL>
 25  *
 26  *  @constructor
 27  */
